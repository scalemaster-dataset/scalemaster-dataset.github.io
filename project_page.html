Abstract

We present MyPointCloud, a large-scale point cloud dataset designed for advancing research in SLAM, 3D reconstruction, and scene understanding. Our dataset contains 15 diverse sequences captured across various indoor and outdoor environments, totaling 45 million points and covering 18.5 GB of high-quality LiDAR and RGB-D data.

Each sequence includes accurate camera trajectories, calibration parameters, and dense point clouds with color information. The dataset features challenging scenarios including dynamic objects, varying lighting conditions, and complex geometric structures, making it ideal for benchmarking state-of-the-art algorithms.

Data Collection Pipeline

Our data collection pipeline consists of synchronized LiDAR and RGB-D sensors mounted on a mobile platform. We use state-of-the-art SLAM algorithms for initial trajectory estimation, followed by global bundle adjustment for millimeter-level accuracy. Each point cloud is automatically labeled with semantic information and verified through manual inspection.
